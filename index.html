Heavy Rains Displace Over 30,000 People Across the Dominican Republic

A trough system combined with strong northeaster winds caused severe flooding, incommunicating communities and damaging thousands of homes in the North, East, and South regions.

Heavy rains and powerful winds swept through the Dominican Republic over the weekend, causing the displacement of more than 30,000 people and flooding over 6,100 homes across multiple provinces. President Luis Abinader declared a regional state of emergency in five provinces and the National District following the severe damage. The National Meteorological Institute (Indomet) warned that the trough system, combined with humid northeaster winds, could generate continued thunderstorms, electrical storms, and wind gusts throughout the day.
