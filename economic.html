Dominican Remittances Surpass $3 Billion in the First Quarter of 2026

A 1.9% increase over the same period last year reflects continued strong economic ties between the Dominican diaspora and the home country.

According to data reported by the Dominican news agency EFE, the Dominican Republic received more than $3.019 billion in remittances during the first quarter of 2026 — a 1.9% increase compared to the first quarter of 2025. Financial experts describe remittances as a critical pillar of the Dominican economy, providing direct support to hundreds of thousands of families.

Economic researchers note that the growth in remittance flows indicates confidence among Dominicans living abroad, particularly in the United States. The Dominican diaspora continues to invest in family support, housing, and small businesses in the country.

"Remittances remain the backbone of household income for many Dominican families and are an indicator of the strong bonds between our diaspora and the homeland."

— Financial Analyst, Santo Domingo, April 2026

By Roberto Núñez, Economics Reporter · April 13, 2026 · Source: AlMomento.net / EFE

Dominican Peso Strengthens Against U.S. Dollar — Rate Drops to RD$60.28

The Dominican peso gained nearly 90 cents against the U.S. dollar on Monday, according to banking sector data, reflecting relative economic stability despite the ongoing emergency.

Economic researchers noted that the currency's stability is partly attributed to strong tourism revenues and consistent remittance flows. Authorities suggest the central bank's monetary policy has played a key role in maintaining exchange rate stability.
