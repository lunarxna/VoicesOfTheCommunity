Heavy Rains and Strong Winds Devastate Communities Across the Dominican Republic

A powerful trough system causes massive flooding, displacing families and isolating entire neighborhoods across the North, East, and South regions.

By Maria Santos, Local Reporter  ·  April 14, 2026  ·  Sources: Listín Diario, AlMomento.net

Emergency crews assist residents displaced by flooding in Santo Domingo neighborhoods. (April 13, 2026)

A powerful weather system swept through the Dominican Republic over the weekend, leaving a trail of destruction across communities in the North, East, and South of the country. According to reports from AlMomento.net and Listín Diario, more than 30,000 people were displaced from their homes, over 6,100 residences were flooded, and at least 14 communities were left completely incommunicado.

The National Meteorological Institute (Indomet) confirmed that the trough system, combined with strong northeaster winds, continued to generate heavy showers, electrical storms, and dangerous gusts throughout Tuesday. Citizens were strongly advised to avoid rivers, streams, and low-lying areas.

"The impact of these rains is one of the most severe we have seen in years. Families need immediate support, and we are responding with all available resources."

— President Luis Abinader, April 13, 2026

President Luis Abinader personally visited the Nuevo Amanecer sector in Los Alcarrizos to supervise relief efforts. He later declared a regional state of emergency in five provinces and the National District, mobilizing the Center for Emergency Operations (COE) and dozens of government institutions to coordinate rescues and provide food, shelter, and medical assistance to affected families.

Local authorities reported significant damage to agricultural areas, roads, and electrical infrastructure. Several vehicles were destroyed by fallen trees and power lines. The COE urged drivers to exercise extreme caution on roadways due to reduced visibility, waterlogged streets, and the risk of sudden flooding.

Community volunteers and civil defense organizations joined the response, providing medical assistance, temporary shelter, and emergency supplies to those most in need. Health officials warned residents to be alert for waterborne illnesses that can follow flooding events, urging people to drink only clean water and report any signs of disease to local health centers.
